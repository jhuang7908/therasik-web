Background & Rationale
Why Re-design CDRs After Affinity Maturation?

The SR-R2 G49A+F112L champion achieves sub-nanomolar binding to HER2 domain IV; however, its CDR sequences carry high similarity to the original murine scaffold — a potential liability for intellectual-property freedom-to-operate (FTO) and immunogenicity profiling. De novo CDR redesign using structure-constrained generative models is increasingly adopted to diversify CDR sequences while preserving the binding scaffold [1,2]. This work systematically quantifies the consequences of that redesign and establishes a re-maturation workflow that recovers affinity without reverting to the original CDR sequences.

Strategic Objectives

  • › Expand CDR sequence space for FTO and patent differentiation
  • › Maintain HER2 domain IV epitope overlap (structural constraint)
  • › Recover binding affinity via physics-guided re-maturation
  • › Reduce CDR-associated immunogenicity risk (NetMHCIIpan T-cell epitope screening)

Clinical Relevance

CDR redesign is particularly relevant for camelid-derived VHHs where the original CDR sequences may carry camelid-specific patterns that trigger ADA responses in humans. Studies have shown that CDR regions contribute disproportionately to T-cell epitope load [3]. Redesigning CDRs with human germline-like sequences, while maintaining affinity, is an emerging strategy for developing "CDR-humanized" nanobodies with improved clinical immunogenicity profiles [4].

Phase II — Structure-Constrained CDR Redesign
Generative AI Redesign on the SR-R2 Scaffold

The SR-R2 HADDOCK3-refined complex PDB was used as the structural template. CDR2 and CDR3 loops were designated for redesign; all framework residues and VHH hallmark positions were frozen. A structure-aware deep learning generative model [1] was applied at multiple sampling temperatures to generate a diverse library of 150+ CDR sequence candidates. Candidates were ranked against four mandatory decision gates.

The Affinity Valley: Interface Contacts Collapsed from 80 to 68

Structure-aware generative models optimize for sequence naturalness and structural plausibility — not for binding energy. Upon CDR redesign, the PRODIGY intermolecular contact count dropped from 80 (SR-R2) → 68 (WT-0838) — a 15% reduction. Simultaneously, salt bridges fell from 92 to 40 and hydrogen bonds from 18 to 7. The MM/GBSA binding energy deteriorated from −63.75 to −32.16 kcal/mol (a +31.6 kcal/mol loss), and PRODIGY Kd degraded 92-fold from 0.54 nM to 49.75 nM.

This "affinity valley" is a predictable consequence of CDR sequence diversification. The new CDR2 sequences (WIDAARGLK) sample a distinct rotameric ensemble that lacks the dense electrostatic network present in SR-R2 (RIYPTNGY). Re-maturation is mandatory before experimental validation of redesigned CDRs.

结构意义 — 保留位点解析

CDR2 保留位点：W52、G54、I56、G63、Y67、P69、K70
W52 是 CDR2 环 N 端起点，大侧链 Trp 通过 π 堆叠维持 loop 入场几何；K70 是 C 端终点，其 ε-氨基参与骨架氢键网络——两者的保留是 CDR2 环结构完整性的前提。G54 和 G63 为 loop 折转提供骨架柔性，含 Cβ 的替换均会引入立体冲突；I56 填充疏水内核；Y67 与框架区形成侧链氢键；P69 的刚性脯氨酸骨架限制 loop 出口角度。这些位点在序列上均保留了亲本残基，反映了各位置的结构敏感性。

CDR3 保留位点：S105、R106、G109、G111、M115、D116
S105 和 R106 是 CDR3 环 N 端的边界位点，M115 和 D116 为 C 端边界位点，四个位点均保留了亲本序列，维持 CDR3 环与框架区的结构连续性。G109 和 G111 是 CDR3 中央的甘氨酸，在 VHH 抗体组库中高度保守 [8]，其保留反映了 Gly 为 loop 主链提供旋转自由度的结构必要性。

CDR backbone RMSD：CDR2 ≤ 0.72 Å · CDR3 ≤ 0.45 Å（vs parent HADDOCK complex）。骨架整体 RMSD ≤ 0.40 Å，确认框架区完全保留，仅 CDR 侧链构象发生调整。

FTO 与专利独立性

CDR2 变化 10 个位点、CDR3 变化 6 个位点，共 16/29 个 CDR 位点发生实质性变化（55% 多态性）。其中 CDR2 核心可变区（PDB 55–68）9 个位点变化，达到独立专利保护通常要求的 >30% 差异化阈值。CDR2 理化性质类别从"富芳环极性"（GRIYPTNGY）转变为"脂肪族-带电"（WIDAARGL 段），构成实质性新颖性，超出单氨基酸替换范畴。

Phase III — Physics-Guided Re-maturation
Recovering the Interface Through Targeted Polar Mutations

A condensed 4-phase VAM pipeline was applied to WT-0838 with CDR1 excluded (no significant binding contribution confirmed by Phase 1 Ala scan). Two critical innovations drove success: (1) restricting Phase 2 scanning to confirmed hotspot positions only, and (2) a polar-priority two-tier gating algorithm — standard mutations require ΔΔG < −0.5 kcal/mol (EvoEF2), while polar substitutions (Arg, Tyr, His) use a relaxed gate of ΔΔG < +3.0 kcal/mol with mandatory PRODIGY recheck [5].

Key Insight: Salt Bridge Recovery via Charged Residue Insertion

L64K (Lys at CDR2 position 64): Standard EvoEF2 ΔΔG = −1.16 kcal/mol (passed standard gate). This Lys residue directly restores an electrostatic interaction with a negatively charged patch on HER2 domain IV (analogous to the lost interface salt bridge network). MM/GBSA recovered +15.2 kcal/mol; n_contacts: 68 → 70; PRODIGY Kd: 49.75 → 17.78 nM.

W55R (Arg at CDR2 position 55): EvoEF2 ΔΔG = +1.94 kcal/mol — this would have been rejected by a standard single-tier gate. PRODIGY recheck confirmed genuine affinity gain (ΔΔG = −0.31 kcal/mol). The Arg guanidinium group enables bifurcated hydrogen bonding at the interface. Combined with L64K, the double mutant achieves MM/GBSA −52.00 kcal/mol and Kd 10.57 nM. This is the key case validating the polar-priority algorithm [5].

Binding Interface Analysis NEW
Contact Residue Mapping, SC, and Epitope Footprint

The binding site analysis addresses three questions: (1) How many interface contacts are maintained? (2) How well do the two surfaces geometrically fit (SC)? (3) Does the CDR redesign shift the HER2 epitope footprint — i.e., do the same antigen residues remain engaged? Contact identity — which HER2 residues are contacted — matters as much as total contact count.

Contact Count: 80 → 68 → ~76

  • SR-R2: 80 total intermolecular contacts (PRODIGY 5.5 Å cutoff). Dense CDR2 contacts via RIYPTNGY motif engaging HER2 β-hairpin (residues ~560–575)
  • WT-0838 Redesign: 68 contacts (−15%). New CDR2 (WIDAARGLK) adopts a shifted rotameric ensemble — the Trp at 55 provides steric bulk but reduced polarity for charged HER2 surface engagement
  • A64K: 70 contacts (+2). Lys at 64 directly engages a negatively charged HER2 patch, restoring one lost salt-bridge pair
  • Super Combo: ~76 contacts (estimated). G49A removes steric conflict; K112L restores hydrophobic groove packing from SR-R2, recovering ~95% of the original contact complement

Shape Complementarity (SC) across Nodes

  • SC (dimensionless, 0–1) measures geometric surface fit at the interface; typical antibody-antigen complexes score 0.64–0.72 [6]
  • SR-R2 (SC ~0.68): above average — compact CDR2 loop packing with good planar complementarity. G49A slightly flattens the CDR2 backbone, further improving fit
  • WT-0838 (SC ~0.58): significant drop. The bulky Trp indole at position 55 creates sub-optimal steric fit with the HER2 surface groove
  • Super Combo (SC ~0.65): near-recovery. W55R replaces the indole with flexible Arg, which samples multiple rotamers for maximal surface contact; G49A further smooths CDR2 curvature

Epitope Footprint: Does the Binding Site Shift?

  • HER2 epitope is conserved across all nodes. All variants maintain contacts with HER2 domain IV key residues (Tyr553, Ser558, Gln560, Asp576, Phe582), enforced by the CDR RMSD ≤ 0.9 Å structural constraint gate during redesign
  • However, the VHH residues making those contacts change: SR-R2 uses Arg52/Ile53/Tyr54 for polar contacts; WT-0838 uses Trp55/Ile51/Asp52 — different CDR geometry engaging the same antigen patch
  • W55R (Double/Super): the new Arg55 re-engages HER2 Asp576 via salt bridge, partly re-establishing the electrostatic network lost during redesign
  • Clinical implication: Preserved HER2 epitope footprint ensures all redesigned variants compete with trastuzumab for HER2 domain IV, maintaining mechanism-of-action specificity

CDR Residue Composition Change

  • CDR2 full loop identity (PDB 52–70, 17 aa): 41% — 7/17 positions conserved. Core region (PDB 54–68, 9 positions): 33% identity (3 conserved: G54, I56, G63). CDR3 full loop identity (PDB 105–116, 12 aa): 50% — 6/12 conserved. Combined CDR2+3 polymorphism: 55% (16/29 positions changed). Backbone RMSD validation: CDR2 0.72 Å, CDR3 0.45 Å — both below the 0.9 Å gate.
  • Salt bridges at interface: 92 (SR-R2) → 40 (WT-0838) — the aromatic-rich RIYPTNGY enabled dense π-cation and H-bond contacts with HER2 that the aliphatic WIDAARGLK cannot fully replicate
  • Re-maturation recovers key electrostatic contacts: the W55R + L64K double mutant re-establishes 2 of the 3 principal lost salt bridges; Super Combo recovers 1 additional hydrophobic packing contact via K112L
  • Net result: Super Combo retains 41 salt bridges — statistically equivalent to WT-0838 baseline, with improved geometric quality (SC 0.65) and contact count (~76)
Future Outlook — CMC Optimization
Framework Region pI Engineering: Targeting pI < 9.0

The Super Combo achieves excellent binding affinity (3.75 nM) but carries pI 9.17 — in the VHH WARN zone (≥9.0) that may complicate high-throughput purification and long-term stability at physiological pH. Framework region (FR) optimization can systematically lower pI without compromising CDR-mediated binding or VHH hallmark stability [7,8].

pI Engineering Strategy: Framework K→T/Q Substitution

Analysis of the Super Combo sequence identifies two candidate FR positions for charge-reducing mutations that are non-hallmark, solvent-exposed, and structurally remote from CDR loops:

K13 → Thr (FR1, position 13)
Solvent-exposed FR1 Lys, non-conserved in VHH germlines. K→T removes +1 charge without introducing hydrophobicity. Estimated pI change: −0.32 units. Structural risk: minimal — no contact with CDRs or antigen.
K19 → Thr (FR1, position 19)
Second FR1 Lys, surface-accessible. K→T: −0.28 pI units. Combined with K13T: projected pI of Super Combo = 8.57 — returning to original humanized VHH baseline. Low structural risk.

Projected outcome: K13T + K19T on Super Combo background: pI 9.17 → ~8.57 (PASS zone), net charge pH7: +4.4 → +2.4. ADI Grade would improve from WARN to PASS on the charge dimension. CMC compatibility significantly enhanced for cation-exchange purification and formulation stability.

Hallmark Safety Check — What NOT to Mutate

VHH framework harbors 6 hallmark residues that are absolutely conserved for single-domain stability: F37, W47, G44, L45, R52, F103 (Chothia numbering). These positions must not be altered. Additionally, K23 (VHH canonical position) and K39 (FR2 structurally important) should be avoided. The proposed K13T + K19T targets are clearly outside all protected zones.

A further option is R85 → Ser (FR3 position, non-hallmark) which would contribute an additional −0.25 pI reduction, bringing the theoretical minimum to pI ~8.30 — well within the optimal VHH CMC zone — without compromising the affinity engineering achieved in Phase III.
