Executive Summary

247 突变扫描数, One Optimal Double Mutant

A 3-minute overview of the problem, approach, and outcome.

The Challenge

VGRW-SR-R2 is a humanized anti-HER2 VHH nanobody with confirmed binding — but affinity improvement is needed for therapeutic potency. Random mutagenesis risks destabilizing the molecule. The challenge: identify mutations that enhance binding energy while preserving thermostability, solubility, and CMC profile.

Our Approach

Therasik's 6-tool ΔΔG consensus pipeline: exhaustive scanning of all interface residues (247 mutations) across 5 filter dimensions — EvoEF2, PRODIGY, ThermoMPNN, AntiFold, and ESM-IF1. Epistatic double-mutant analysis to discover synergistic combinations invisible to single-point screening.

Key Outcome

Best single mutant: ΔΔG −3.32 kcal/mol. Epistatic double mutant: −24.0 kcal/mol synergy. Backbone RMSD preserved at 0.000 Å. Interface contacts increased by 70%. All candidates passed thermostability and CMC filters — ready for experimental validation.

Background

VGRW-SR-R2: HER2-Targeting Nanobody Optimization

VGRW-SR-R2 is a humanized VHH single-domain antibody engineered against HER2 (ErbB2), a validated oncology target overexpressed in breast, gastric, and lung cancers. Nanobody affinity is critically dependent on three CDRs and adjacent framework residues. Enhancing binding affinity enables improved tumor penetration and dosing flexibility — but mutations must not compromise thermal stability, expressibility, or CMC developability.

Exhaustive Interface Scanning

All 13 high-contact interface residues tested against every possible substitution — no hand-picked hot spots, no human selection bias.

5-Dimensional Filtering

Binding energy, thermal stability, evolutionary fitness, sequence compatibility, and CMC developability — candidates must pass all five dimensions to advance.

Epistatic Discovery

G49A and F112L each showed unfavorable energy in isolation — but combined, they produced the only double mutant to outperform wild type by strong synergy.

Phase 5 · Discovery

Epistatic Synergy Uncovered

G49A and F112L individually showed unfavorable binding energy (+13.20 and +7.48 kcal/mol vs WT). Combined, they produced the only double mutant to outperform wild type — a textbook positive epistasis case.

Cooperative Energy Gain

G49A fills a local cavity in the HER2 interface with its methyl group, restructuring the microenvironment around F112. F112L then replaces a polar-mismatched phenylalanine with leucine, increasing hydrophobic interface contacts from 20 to 34 pairs (+70%).

Methodology

Prediction Reliability

A candidate is designated "high confidence" only when binding energy, thermal stability, evolutionary fitness, and CMC metrics all converge. Results are directional guides — wet-lab validation is required before clinical advancement.

Accuracy Benchmarks

  • Fast binding energy screen: r ≈ 0.45–0.58 (directional filtering)
  • All-atom physics calculation: r ≈ 0.55–0.70 (final ranking)
  • Thermal stability: qualitative veto gate
  • Evolutionary fitness: qualitative veto gate

Interpretation Notes

  • Confirm with SPR (BIAcore) or BLI (Octet) vs HER2 ECD
  • ΔΔG < ±2 kcal/mol not significant without experimental data
  • Cross-batch absolute values are not comparable
  • Next step: G49A+F112L + WT synthesis, parallel CHO/yeast expression
