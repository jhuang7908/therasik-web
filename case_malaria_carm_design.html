Executive Summary

A Rationally Designed CAR-Macrophage for High-Parasitemia Malaria

A 3-minute overview of the problem, approach, and outcome.

The Challenge

P. falciparum malaria kills >600,000 people annually. In high-parasitemia cases (>10% iRBC), existing drugs take 24–48h to clear — during which organ failure progresses. Infected red blood cells are anucleate, making CAR-T cell therapy impossible (T cells kill via caspase-3 apoptosis, which requires a nucleus).

Our Approach

Therasik's ACTES 11-layer decision cascade: systematically evaluated 5 blood-stage antigens → selected CIDRα1 (PfEMP1) as the only viable target. Designed a dual-binder tandem scFv (C7 + C74) covering all 6 CIDRα1 subclasses. FcRγ-based signaling for macrophage-native phagocytosis. mRNA-LNP delivery targeting splenic macrophages.

Key Outcome

Covers all 6 CIDRα1 subclasses with structurally validated binders. <1 hour mRNA-LNP deployment timeline. A 4-tier wet lab strategy from C57BL/6 mechanism proof through NHP IND-enabling studies. Var gene switching analysis shows antigen escape is self-attenuating in malaria — unlike tumor antigen loss.

Disease Biology

Why Macrophages, Not T Cells?

Infected red blood cells (iRBCs) in P. falciparum malaria are 7–8 μm anucleate cells. T cell killing relies on Caspase-3 apoptosis — which requires a nucleus. Phagocytosis by macrophages is the only validated clearance mechanism.

Preclinical

4-Tier Wet Lab Strategy

Each tier answers a distinct question. P. falciparum exclusively infects human red blood cells, constraining model selection.

Tier 1: C57BL/6

Mechanism proof. CIDRα1-coated murine RBC. No BSL-3 required. ~3 weeks timeline.

Tier 2: MISTRG6

Human macrophage delivery validation. >80% human macrophages in spleen. No Pf infection needed.

Tier 3: NBSGW

P. falciparum efficacy. Human CD34+ HSC engraftment. Human RBC 1–5%. IT4 strain infection.

Tier 4: NHP

IND-enabling. P. knowlesi in cynomolgus macaque. GLP tox + biodistribution.

Risk Analysis

Var Gene Switching — A Self-Limiting Escape

Unlike tumor antigen escape (where antigen-negative tumors retain full lethality), malaria antigen escape is self-attenuating.

Key Insight: CIDRα1 is the virulence-determining domain. Parasites that escape CAR-M by switching to non-CIDRα1 var genes lose EPCR binding → lose brain sequestration → lose severe pathology. The immune evasion paradox works IN FAVOR of the patient.
