01 · The Problem

What C-Domain Engineering Does Not Assess

IgG-like bispecific platforms solve chain assembly — but "correctly assembled" does not mean "correctly paired at the variable domain level."

#ProblemLocationConsequenceSolved by KiH / CrossMab?
1HC Heterodimerization CH3 domain Homodimer contamination ✓ Yes (KiH T366W/S)
2LC Mispairing CH1–CL Wrong LC on wrong Fab arm Partial — requires Common LC or CrossMab
3VH/VL Domain Selectivity Variable domain interface Suboptimal CDR geometry; potential crossover pairing in flexible formats ✗ Not assessed

Faricimab context: Faricimab (Vabysmo®, Roche/Genentech, FDA-approved 2022) is the first approved ocular bispecific antibody targeting both VEGF-A and ANG-2. It uses Knob-into-Hole (KiH) for HC heterodimerization plus CrossFab (CH1/CL domain swap on the VEGF-A arm) for LC selectivity. Unlike DuoBody® (Fab-arm exchange, post-expression), Faricimab is produced by co-expression — all four chains are co-translated, making VH/VL variable domain selectivity a genuine assembly requirement. The CrossFab C-domain engineering addresses LC-level mispairing but does not directly constrain the VH-VL variable domain interface. Whether Fv-level selectivity provides additional protection is the question VPSA addresses.

Key finding: Neither arm has engineered VH44/VL100 electrostatic steering (a deliberate Lewis pair like VH44E/VL100K). Arm A's selectivity emerges from framework-level salt bridge complementarity (VH3/Vκ canonical interface: 156 contacts). Arm B (VH1/Vλ) relies on CrossFab constant-domain architecture as the primary selectivity mechanism, with the Fv level providing only modest backup (63 SB). This highlights a fundamental principle: CrossFab architecture is most valuable precisely when Fv-level selectivity is insufficient.

04 · Interpretation

Arm-Specific Selectivity — Why BSA Alone Misleads

Faricimab demonstrates a key VPSA lesson: crossover BSA can exceed native BSA (a model geometry artifact), but salt bridge counts reliably reveal the underlying electrostatic complementarity difference.

VH3/Vκ is the most prevalent human antibody germline combination, evolved for tight VH-VL packing. 156 salt bridges at the native interface reflects this; the 72% drop when VEGF-VH is paired with ANG2-VL (λ) reveals the structural incompatibility between VH3 and lambda VL framework geometry.

BSA actually increases in the crossover pair (H2L1 > H2L2), which would incorrectly predict the crossover as more stable. Only the salt bridge count (63 → 19, −70%) correctly identifies the native pair preference — validating multi-metric VPSA over BSA-only screening.

Why Faricimab still works clinically despite Arm B's lower Fv selectivity: CrossFab (CH1/CL swap on Arm A) forces LC-level selectivity: the VEGF-A LC (with CH1 instead of CL) cannot complete a stable Fab with the ANG-2 HC (which expects a standard CL). This C-domain architecture makes the primary assembly gate, allowing Arm B to function even with modest Fv-level VH-VL selectivity. The VPSA finding does not indicate a product quality problem — it correctly maps the two layers of selectivity: Arm A has dual protection (Fv electrostatics + CrossFab), Arm B relies primarily on CrossFab. For future designs without CrossFab (e.g., KiH only with two kappa arms), Arm B-level Fv selectivity would be insufficient and electrostatic steering mutations would be required.

📏

When Fv Selectivity Is Critical

KiH-only bispecifics with two kappa (or two lambda) arms — no CrossFab backup. Also critical in Fab fragments, scFv-BiTEs, VHH-Fv fusions, and any format without constant-domain selectivity.

⚡

The Electrostatic Steering Solution

For arms with low Fv-level salt bridge count, adding VH44E + VL100K (Lewis charge pair) introduces a directed electrostatic lock. Especially critical when both arms use the same LC isotype (kappa/kappa) and similar VH germlines.

🔬

Dry-Wet Validation Path

Fab fragments of each arm tested by thermal shift assay (DSF) with equimolar competing VL. A well-selective arm (Arm A type) shows minimal Tm shift; a low-selectivity arm (Arm B type) shows measurable destabilization — confirming the VPSA prediction.

05 · CMC & Immunogenicity Impact

What Low Fv Selectivity Actually Costs

Insufficient VH/VL pairing selectivity is not just a structural curiosity — it has direct, quantifiable consequences for manufacturing, stability, and patient safety.

⚠️ Critical in chronic dosing regimens — ADA incidence is cumulative across injection cycles. Even minor crossover fractions become significant immunogenic stimulus over 6–12 months.
06 · V-Region Mutation Design

VH44/VL100 Electrostatic Steering

When VPSA identifies INSUFFICIENT selectivity — or when defense-in-depth is required — InSynBio designs Lewis charge pairs at VH44/VL100 to introduce a directed electrostatic lock at the buried Fv interface hub.

Defense-in-Depth Principle: Even when CrossFab is already deployed, adding VH44/VL100 mutations provides a second independent selectivity layer. CrossFab addresses LC-level mispairing (C-domain gate); VH44/VL100 charge pairs address Fv-level selectivity (V-domain gate). Mispairing then requires simultaneous failure of both layers — dramatically less probable, and especially critical when CrossFab is partially compromised by reduction or degradation during manufacturing.

Target Positions — Why VH44 and VL100

VH Kabat 44
  • Located in FR2, buried at VH-VL interface
  • SASA < 10 Ų in properly folded Fv
  • Not in any CDR loop — mutation does not affect antigen binding
  • Faricimab: both arms G (neutral) — no engineered steering
VL Kabat 100
  • Located in FR4 region, packed against VH44
  • SASA < 10 Ų — buried, tolerated for charge mutation
  • Not in CDR3 L (boundary is Kabat 89–97)
  • Faricimab Arm A: Q (neutral); Arm B: G (neutral)

Dual Orthogonal Lewis Pairs — Electrostatic Logic
Dual orthogonal design: Arm A carries VH44E + VL100K; Arm B carries VH44K + VL100E. Both native pairs are electrostatically favored (opposite charges attract). Both crossover combinations experience same-charge repulsion — thermodynamically disfavored at the Fv level independent of C-domain architecture.

6-Step Mutation Validation Pipeline

1
SASA Check — Verify VH44 and VL100 both SASA < 15 Ų in native structure. If SASA > 20 Ų: consider alternative positions.
2
EvoEF2 ΔΔG — Single-point mutation ΔΔG at VH44/VL100. Acceptable: ΔΔG < +2.5 kcal/mol. Buried charge pairs compensate via partner attraction.
3
BES Proxy Recalculation — Recompute salt bridge count and BES for all 4 pairs after in silico mutation. Confirm native pair SB increase ≥ 20; crossover SB stable or decreased.
4
Immunogenicity Rescan — NetMHCpan / IEDB API scan of 9-mers and 15-mers containing mutated positions across top 6 HLA alleles. Flag any new binding score < 0.5% rank.
5
AF2 Multimer Validation — Full bispecific structure after mutation. Confirm VH-VL packing angle ± 5° vs. parent; CDR3 Cα RMSD < 0.5 Å; no new interface clashes.
6
Experimental Validation Plan — DSF thermal shift with competing VL (confirm selectivity gain); SEC (no new aggregation peak); SPR/BLI antigen Kd within 3-fold of parent.

Faricimab Application: Arm B (VH1/Vλ, 63 SB) has MODERATE Fv selectivity. For a hypothetical KiH-only redesign of ANG-2 Arm B with same-isotype kappa arms — no CrossFab protection — the recommended fix would be Arm B VH44K + VL100E paired with Arm A VH44E + VL100K (dual orthogonal). This converts Arm B from reliance on CrossFab to having independent Fv-level selectivity. The 6-step validation pipeline above would confirm whether the charge pair introduces ≥ 20 additional SB in the native pairing and < 0.5% rank immunogenic peptides.

VPSA Scope and Limitations

What VPSA Measures
  • Interface BSA (FreeSASA, Fv-only)
  • Interface H-bond and salt bridge counts
  • BES proxy (weighted energy estimate)
  • VH44 / VL100 Kabat residue identity and charge
  • Selectivity classification (STRONG / MODERATE / INSUFFICIENT)

Limitations (current version)
  • BES proxy ≠ thermodynamic ΔΔG (EvoEF2 required for quantitative ΔΔG)
  • Structures are predicted (IgFold/ABodyBuilder2), not experimentally determined
  • Assembly kinetics not modeled (thermodynamics only)
