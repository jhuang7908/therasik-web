Executive Summary

Diagnosing pI-Driven ER Retention, Rescuing Yeast Expression

A 3-minute overview of the problem, approach, and outcome.

The Challenge

A client's bispecific VHH–GS–VHH construct targeting both MERS-CoV and SARS-CoV-1 showed potent dual neutralization in vitro — but failed in Pichia pastoris yeast expression. The secreted yield was too low for manufacturing scale-up, and the root cause was unknown.

Our Approach

Therasik's SmartLink™ + VirtualCMC pipeline: systematic analysis of 12 VHH variants across pI, net charge, hydrophobicity, and linker composition. Diagnosed pI-driven ER retention as the bottleneck — the fusion's high pI caused aggregation in the yeast secretory pathway. Designed charged-linker alternatives to rescue secretion.

Key Outcome

Reduced pI by 1.09 units and net charge by 4.0 units through arm selection + charged-linker engineering. Achieved 4.8× improvement in cross-coronavirus neutralization activity. Expression rescued with no loss of binding function — ready for manufacturing scale-up.

Background

A Potent Format, a Persistent Expression Bottleneck

The client developed a VHH-GS-VHH bispecific nanobody targeting two distinct coronavirus spike proteins. Despite confirmed dual neutralization activity in monovalent format, the fused construct showed consistently low expression yield in yeast — limiting downstream development.

🎯 Project Objective

Diagnose the molecular basis of low yeast expression in a VHH-GS-VHH bispecific fusion and provide a sequence-level engineering solution that preserves — or improves — broad coronavirus neutralization activity.

🧬 Format

Bispecific nanobody: αCoV-A VHH — (G₄S)₃ linker — αCoV-B VHH. Two humanized VHH arms from independent anti-coronavirus programs, fused via a flexible glycine-serine linker. Expressed in yeast (Pichia pastoris / S. cerevisiae).

📦 Input Data

Multiple humanized VHH variants for each arm, with full sequences and pseudovirus neutralization IC50/IC90 data across a panel of viral variant strains. All molecular identifiers and sequence data are confidential.

Client Constraint: Target identities, sequences, and proprietary assay details are confidential. All molecular names are anonymized as αCoV-A / αCoV-B; individual variant designators have been removed. This case study presents methodology and anonymized numeric results only.

Stage 1 — Individual VHH CMC
Computed 15 physicochemical metrics for all 12 VHH variants using BioPython ProteinAnalysis (pI, GRAVY, instability index, net charge) and sequence-pattern scanning (deamidation NG/NS motifs, isomerization DG/DS, oxidation M/W sites). Benchmarked against VHH42 clinical reference database (n=42 clinical/late-stage humanized nanobodies) using tent-function continuous ADI scoring.
Stage 2 — Expression Root-Cause Analysis
All αCoV-A variants clustered at pI 8.99–9.01; the original αCoV-B arm showed pI = 8.59. The initial fusion (original arm pair + (G₄S)₃) computed to pI = 8.94, net charge +5.0 at pH 7. ER lumen pH ≈ 7.2; a construct with pI 8.94 retains a net positive charge of approximately +5 under ER conditions, promoting electrostatic adsorption to luminal chaperones (BiP/GRP78, calreticulin) and negatively charged membranes — the mechanistic basis for secretory pathway retention and subsequent ERAD-mediated degradation.
Stage 3 — Arm Selection Optimization
Neutralization IC50/IC90 data across virus panels enabled rigorous arm selection. For αCoV-A, the selected variant uniquely achieved full breadth coverage (IC50 ≤ 0.037 µg/mL across all four tested strains) while runner-up variants lost potency against one or more strains. For αCoV-B, cross-coronavirus IC90 analysis revealed a >4-fold potency differential between the selected and original variants — 0.025 µg/mL vs. 0.119 µg/mL on the critical cross-reactive strain.
Stage 4 — SmartLink™ Charged Linker Engineering
Five linker designs were evaluated by sequence-level pI computation of the full fusion. Three C-terminal glutamate additions to (G₄S)₃ — yielding the (G₄S)₃+3E linker — reduced fusion pI by 0.95 units to 7.85 and net charge from +4.0 to +1.0. Critically, the commonly cited Whitlow linker was found to increase pI (to 8.93) due to its lysine content, and was rejected.

Root Cause Analysis

pI-Driven ER Electrostatic Retention

The original arm pairing fused with a standard (G₄S)₃ linker was screened for its full-fusion physicochemical profile. The results revealed a compounding charge problem that directly explains low secretion yield.

"When a protein's isoelectric point exceeds the pH of the secretory pathway lumen (~7.2 in the ER), it carries a net positive charge that promotes non-specific electrostatic adsorption to luminal chaperones and negatively charged membranes — slowing translocation, extending dwell time, and increasing susceptibility to ERAD-mediated degradation." — Therasik VirtualCMC Expression Module, mechanistic rationale

SmartLink™ Engineering

Charged Linker Design — pI as a Manufacturing Lever

Each glutamate residue added to the C-terminus of the (G₄S)₃ linker contributes approximately −1.0 unit of net charge at pH 7, translating to a 0.35–0.45 unit reduction in fusion pI. The linker segment — physically separated from both CDR regions — is the ideal site for charge engineering with minimal risk to antigen binding.

Viral Strains Covered

The (G₄S)₃+3E linker deposits three glutamate residues at the C-terminus of the flexible spacer — distal from both CDR regions — reducing fusion pI by 1.09 units and net charge by 4.0 units, while the arm substitution delivers a co-incidental 4.8× gain in cross-coronavirus breadth.

Expression (Predicted)

Fusion pI 7.85 places the construct below the ER electrostatic retention threshold (~8.5). Net charge reduced from +5.0 to +1.0 at pH 7 minimizes non-specific membrane and chaperone interactions in the secretory pathway.

Activity — Dual Coronavirus

The selected αCoV-A arm maintains IC50 ≤ 0.037 µg/mL across all four tested viral strains — uniquely achieving full-panel breadth coverage. The selected αCoV-B arm achieves cross-coronavirus IC90 = 0.025 µg/mL, the best performer across the evaluated panel by >4-fold.

CMC Stability Risks

Flagged risks: the selected αCoV-A arm carries 3 deamidation-susceptible NG/NS motifs (long-term stability concern); the αCoV-B scaffold carries above-median oxidation site count (M/W, structural and shared across the panel). Recommended mitigation: forced-oxidation stress assay + site-directed N→Q evaluation for deamidation.

Experimental Validation Plan

Recommended parallel expression of three constructs: original combination (control), optimized arm pair with (G₄S)₃+3E linker (primary), and backup candidate with same linker. SEC + SDS-PAGE yield comparison, followed by dual-target neutralization assay on the fusion format.
